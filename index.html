Other/Creative Work

ByrdBot—a dynamic web application, written in JavaScript, which sources bird songs from the   Cornell Lab of Ornithology’s archives to sonically represent differences in anthropogenic        species displacement in New Jersey between 1970 and 2017 and 2065. The project was      developed in collaboration with Dr. Charles Schutte (Environmental Science, Rowan       University) as well as undergraduate students from Communication Studies and   Environmental Science. Physical installations of ByrdBot were placed in the Chamberlain

            Student Center, Bozorth Hall, and Science Hall at Rowan University, February 2023-Present. 

 

                        Project URL:    https://soundsofhumanimpact.github.io/byrdbot/ 

 

                        Write Ups:       https://today.rowan.edu/news/2022/06/they-call-it-the-byrd-bot.-                        listen-to-it-tweet..html   

 

                                                https://www.scicommbites.org/post/byrdbot-simulated-tweets-to-                        communicate-climate-change

 

feels_bot—an interactive exhibit constructed from JavaScript that utilizes artificial intelligence,

crowdsourced .gifs, and custom-designed sounds to “catch feels” from users (e.g., to identify and respond to emotions detected from users). Physically installed as part of the “Pull Request” art exhibition in the Vachon Gallery at Seattle University, September-October 2018.

 

Write Up:         https://www.seattleu.edu/artsci/about/news/pull-request-collaborative-                                      code-art.html
