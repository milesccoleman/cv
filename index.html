Courses in Development
Rhetoric of Science, Technology, and
Medicine: “Scientists, technologists, and medical professionals are often thought of
as persons who deal in “facts” more than “persuasion.” This course
interrogates strategies, genres, and common lines of argument that exist
in technical scientific manuscripts, public health messages, and
technology marketing materials. Students marshal their learning of key
thinkers and theories within the area of rhetoric of science, technology,
and medicine (RSTM) to compose a rhetorical criticism of a given
scientific, technological, or medical communication. Emphasis is given
to material understandings of identity and ideology in scientific
discourses.”
Expected Completion: Fall 2019
Software Rhetoric: “Introduction to the interdisciplinary study of software as a cultural
object. History of computing, basic computing concepts, and a beginner’s
introduction to coding are covered. Students draw on rhetorical theory to
practice “reading” the values out of algorithms, databases, and internet
protocols while formulating analyses of the impact and ethics of
software. The course presumes no prior knowledge of software or of
rhetoric.”
Expected Completion: Spring 2020
